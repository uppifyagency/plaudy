Finding residui, ranked (tutti verificati su codice reale, non speculazione)
🔴 Critici
C1 — L'incidente può ripresentarsi: l'idle-unloader può scaricare il modello tra il check e la trascrizione. ensure_model_ready viene chiamato una volta in session.rs:916, poi seguono minuti di lavoro non-engine (lettura PCM da ~170 MB, diarizzazione dell'intera traccia) durante i quali last_activity non viene mai toccato; il timeout di unload di default è 5 minuti e il watcher non considera le sessioni come "recording". Se l'eviction cade lì, transcribe non ricarica il modello — erra ("Model is not loaded for transcription") → riga Failed vuota, identica a oggi. Variante peggiore su sessione dual: la traccia 2 viene persa in silenzio e la riga risulta Done con metà meeting mancante.
C2 — La diarizzazione è il prossimo incidente da 90 minuti. Il fix ha chunked l'ASR ma diarization.rs:217-243 passa ancora l'intera traccia in una singola chiamata a sherpa-onnx, che internamente usa finestre da 10 s con shift di 1 s (hardcoded nel C++): ogni sample passa 10 volte nel modello di segmentazione → per 89 min sono ~15 ore di audio-equivalente di inferenza sequenziale, cioè un finalize inchiodato sulla CPU per decine di minuti con la riga ferma su "transcribing", zero progress e zero cancel — l'utente lo legge come hang, chiude l'app, e il recovery marca la riga Failed. In più il clustering alloca una matrice O(n²) in f64: gestibile a 89 min (~150–330 MB), 0,4–1,3 GB a 3 ore, OOM concreto sulle sessioni all-day che l'auto-capture per definizione abilita. Questa strada non è mai stata percorsa live: oggi il finalize falliva prima, e l'heal trascrive flat senza diarizzare.
C3 — Il doppio engine ha ancora due porte aperte. Cambiare modello dalle Settings (models.rs:147) durante una run lunga carica un secondo engine ignorando il gate; a fine run il vecchio engine sovrascrive quello nuovo nello slot → da lì in poi trascrivi silenziosamente col modello sbagliato. Il watcher inoltre continua a emettere eventi "unloaded" durante le run lunghe → UI e stato desincronizzati.
🟠 Alti
A1 — Le righe "silenzio legittimo" vengono ritrascritte per intero a OGNI avvio. "No speech" resta failed+vuoto e l'heal le riseleziona per sempre (nessun contatore tentativi, nessuno stato terminale) — oggi le entry 12–20 sono state rifatte a entrambi i boot; una riga silenziosa da 89 min costerebbe ~7,5 min di inferenza a ogni lancio dell'app.
A2 — L'heal affama la dettatura. Il gate è tenuto per l'intera run chunked (7–20 min per una riga lunga): una dettatura di 5 secondi lanciata in quel momento resta in coda fino alla fine — il paste arriva minuti dopo.
A3 — Heal e retry perdono gli speaker. Ritrascrivono flat, non diarizzano mai e non toccano i segments salvati: la riga meeting perde le etichette Me/Speaker (e segments vecchi possono restare incoerenti col testo nuovo). Conseguenza concreta per te: l'entry 21 recuperata oggi non ha attribuzioni speaker — l'audio è salvo nel WAV, quindi è recuperabile con un retry diarization-aware che oggi non esiste in UI.
A4 — Il supervisor auto-capture è panicato 7+ volte oggi (Failed to initialize store: Tauri(BadResourceId) nel tick). Il catch_unwind lo recupera, ma è un bug vivo da radicare.
🟡 Medi
* M1 — Recovery all'avvio: un thread per sessione crashata in parallelo + heal concorrente → (N+1) buffer full-audio da ~350 MB e N runtime di diarizzazione contemporanei, nel momento peggiore (boot).
* M2 — Junk micro-sessioni: oggi l'auto-capture ha creato righe ogni ~20 s da audio ambientale (entry 23–25 in 2 minuti). Nota di calibrazione: uno degli audit temeva la frammentazione dei meeting sulle pause >~4,8 s, ma l'evidenza di oggi dice che il meeting ha tenuto 89 minuti filati — il churn reale è sui trigger spuri, non sulla tenuta.
* M3 — Transienti RAM evitabili nel finalize: fs::read + conversione f32 (~513 MB) e la copia per il lead-pad (~684 MB) per traccia a 89 min; raddoppiano a 3 h. Riducibili con decode a chunk e passando l'offset come timestamp invece di materializzare silenzio.
* M4 — Nessun guard per-riga sul retry: doppio click (o retry manuale durante l'heal della stessa riga) = doppio lavoro completo e due Vec full-audio in RAM; la riga inoltre non passa mai a transcribing durante la run (lo spinner è solo stato locale del componente).
⚪ Bassi (elenco secco)
Canali mpsc audio senza backpressure (solo patologico); gate avvelenato → is_model_loaded true per sempre (oggi irraggiungibile, ma è una mina); tagli chunk potenzialmente mid-word su audio senza pause (nessun overlap/dedup al confine); righe con WAV cancellato ritentate a ogni boot; riga vuota senza errori marcata Done (esclusa per sempre dall'heal); crash tra scrittura WAV e creazione riga → audio orfano senza riga; commento stale su mix_tracks e transcript completo loggato a INFO.
Cosa è risultato genuinamente solido
Il capture path è pulito e verificato riga per riga (sink streaming, resampler bounded, VAD a stato fisso, tick del supervisor senza accumuli) — confermato anche empiricamente dagli 89 minuti a RAM piatta. stream_mix_to_wav è ~0,6 MB a prescindere dalla durata. chunk_spans è corretto su tutti gli edge case (niente underflow, niente loop infiniti, span contigui, zero copie). Il protocollo condvar/gate non ha deadlock né lost-wakeup. Il frontend non ha leak di listener né polling, e la History è paginata. E soprattutto: il trigger esatto dell'incidente (slot vuoto durante inferenza → secondo engine) è chiuso dal gate — verificato che non esiste più alcuna finestra in cui slot e gate sono entrambi liberi durante una run.

* mix_tracks no longer exists; only a stale doc reference at session.rs:1095. The mixer is stream_mix_to_wav.

Diarization internals (verified in sherpa-onnx v1.13.3 source, which the app links)
* Window = 160,000 samples (10 s) from model metadata (export-onnx.py:97); window_shift is hardcoded in C++ to 0.1 × window_size = 1 s (offline-speaker-segmentation-pyannote-model.cc:96). → 90% overlap: every sample runs through the segmentation model 10×. 89 min → 5,331 chunks = 14.8 hours of audio-equivalent inference, sequential.
* All per-window segmentation matrices (589×7 f32 each) are held at once (offline-speaker-diarization-pyannote-impl.h:99): 88 MB @ 89 min, 178 MB @ 3 h — linear.
* Embeddings: one row per (chunk, active-speaker) pair, sequential streams, no full-audio copy (ComputeEmbeddings feeds pointer ranges). n ≈ 5–9 k @ 89 min (2-speaker), hard cap 3×chunks = 16 k.
* Clustering: fast-clustering.cc allocates a condensed pairwise matrix std::vector<double> distance(n(n−1)/2) — O(n²) in f64, then complete-linkage hclust. n=6 k → 144 MB; n=9 k → 324 MB; @ 3 h n≈10–18 k → 0.4–1.3 GB; @ 6 h (worst case 3 speakers/chunk, n≈65 k) → up to 17 GB = hard OOM.
* No full-waveform copy or resample inside Process (input is already 16 kHz).
Ranked findings
1. HIGH — finalize wall-time freeze, not RAM, is the next 90-min incident. diarization.rs:217-243 + sherpa's fixed 10× window overlap: an 89-min track pushes ~15 h of audio-equivalent through the segmentation model plus thousands of sequential 10-s embedding extractions, single-threaded, on top of the ASR pass. Expect finalize to be CPU-pegged for tens of minutes (hardware-dependent estimate) with the row stuck on "transcribing". Failure mode: user assumes a hang and quits → recovery sees WAV exists → CleanupArchived → row Failed, transcript lost and must be fully redone. At 3 h this plausibly exceeds an hour. There is no progress reporting (sherpa's progress callback is not wired) and no cancellation.
2. HIGH — O(duration²) clustering memory in sherpa's FastClustering. Invisible from diarization.rs (no knobs set on FastClusteringConfig). Fine @ 89 min (~150-330 MB), heavy @ 3 h (0.4–1.3 GB f64), OOM at the all-day auto-captured session (5-6 h+) the auto-capture feature explicitly enables. Mitigation lives in our code: diarize in bounded (e.g. 30-min) windows and stitch, or cap session length, or set num_clusters/pre-merge embeddings.
3. MED — whole-track f32 load: read_pcm_i16 (session.rs:1154) + lead-pad copy (session.rs:960-963). Peak transients of 513 MB/684 MB @ 89 min and 1.04/1.38 GB @ 3 h per track, resident 342/691 MB held throughout diarize+ASR. Not fatal alone, but it is the base of every stage's peak and trivially reducible: stream-decode i16→f32 in chunks (kills the fs::read double), and pass the lead offset to ASR as a timestamp shift instead of materializing prepended silence (kills the 2× pad copy).
4. MED — solo mic sessions are fully diarized too. label_as_me is only true for the mic track of a dual session (session.rs:979); a 90-min single-source mic recording pays the entire finding-1/2 cost even though it's one known voice. Cheap win: skip or window diarization for solo mic.
5. MED — recovery fan-out: one thread per crashed session, concurrently (session.rs:576, stop() similarly at 515). K crashed long sessions after a reboot finalize in parallel: ASR is serialized by inference_gate (transcription.rs:540), but the 342-691 MB sample buffers and the sherpa engines are not — K× stage-5 peaks simultaneously, plus N ONNX runtimes. Serialize recovery.
6. LOW — stale doc/comment: session.rs:1095 references the deleted mix_tracks; transcription.rs:813 logs the full multi-hundred-KB transcript at info level.
Genuinely bounded and safe (verified)
* Capture: streaming sink never accumulates (recorder.rs:79-90); PCM writer flushes per frame.
* stream_mix_to_wav: strictly sequential chunked reads, ~0.6 MB regardless of duration (session.rs:1097-1149).
* ASR chunking: 300-s windows with quiet-point cuts (chunk_spans, transcription.rs:846-873); chunks are slices, no copies; segments merged with O(total) memory.
* merge_segments O(n log n), align/drop_bleed O(n·m) over segments (≈2-5 k × 2-5 k cheap integer overlap checks, text compare only on time-overlapping pairs) — milliseconds even at 3 h, memory trivial.
* write_segments is a single transaction (history.rs:199-201); get_session_overviews batched.
* Sherpa embedding extraction: per-pair streams over pointer ranges, no whole-file copy.
Bottom line: the "bounded-RAM finalize" claim holds for what it was scoped to (never two tracks at once, streamed mix). The next incident at 90 min is not OOM but a 20-60-min CPU-pegged finalize dominated by 10×-overlapped diarization that users will read as a hang; the OOM is real but arrives via the quadratic f64 clustering matrix plus the whole-track f32 buffers on 3-6 h auto-captured sessions.
A) RAM/resource growth DURING the 89-minute capture — mostly CLEAN, with three residuals
Key numbers used below: 89 min = 5,340 s ≈ 85.4M samples/track at mono 16 kHz → 171 MB on-disk i16 PCM per track, 342 MB as Vec<f32> per track. Disk write rate ~32 KB/s per track; channel traffic ~64 KB/s (16 kHz f32 frames) / ~192 KB/s pre-resample at 48 kHz.
CLEAN (verified, not the RAM culprit):
* handy/src-tauri/src/managers/session.rs:773-793 (write_pcm_stream) — frames go straight to BufWriter + per-frame flush; only a u64 counter accumulates. The teardown log at session.rs:671 ("captured N samples") is that cumulative disk counter, not a buffer (89 min ≈ 85.4M).
* handy/src-tauri/src/audio_toolkit/audio/recorder.rs:79-101 — FrameSink::Streaming never touches processed_samples (out_buf stays empty; regression-pinned by the test at recorder.rs:559). Dictation-mode accumulation only runs while a dictation is recording.
* handy/src-tauri/src/audio_toolkit/audio/resampler.rs — FrameResampler bounded: in_buf ≤ 1024 samples, pending ≤ 1 frame. Visualizer FFT window fixed (≤ 2048).
* handy/src-tauri/src/managers/session.rs:103-199 — AudioActivity is one Mutex<(Option<Instant>, Instant)>; ActivityTap holds one RMS scalar or one fixed-state Silero VAD. No history kept.
* handy/src-tauri/src/audio_toolkit/audio/mic_sensor.rs — sensor holds one Option<Instant>; it is stopped for the whole session anyway (auto_capture.rs:467-472).
* handy/src-tauri/src/managers/auto_capture.rs:238-348 — Supervisor::tick state is 5 fixed fields; the loop's only extras are a Option<(bool,bool)> edge latch and a per-tick Settings clone (freed each tick). Nothing accrues over 89 min.
* Silero VAD (audio_toolkit/vad/silero.rs, smoothed.rs) — fixed hidden state; frame_buffer capped at prefill_frames + 1 (smoothed.rs:44).
* system_audio.rs — IOProc allocates one small Vec per callback (documented); CoreAudio objects torn down on every exit path.
* Whisper/Parakeet engine between dictation uses: held resident by design with the idle unloader; inference checks it out and puts it back — no growth mechanism found.
Residual findings:
1. [HIGH] Diarization is still a whole-file, unchunked inference at finalize — session.rs:983 → managers/diarization.rs:217-243: diarizer.diarize(&samples) hands the full 85.4M-sample system track to ONE sherpa-onnx OfflineSpeakerDiarization::process() call. The uncommitted fix chunked the ASR but not this path; sherpa's segmentation/embedding/clustering over 89 min of audio holds the full f32 track plus its own internal copies and an all-pairs clustering matrix, and it also re-loads the pyannote + embedding models on every call (fresh create(&config) per diarize). Failure scenario: the next long auto-captured meeting finalizes fine on ASR RAM but spikes multi-GB (and takes very long) inside diarize before ASR even runs.
2. [MEDIUM] Finalize/retry hold one full track (+1 transient copy) in RAM — session.rs:957-963 (read_pcm_i16 then padded = vec![0.0; lead]; padded.extend(raw) briefly holds ~2× = up to ~700 MB for a padded 89-min track) and commands/history.rs:128 (read_wav_samples → 342 MB). Bounded and documented ("ONE track at a time"), but it is the buffer behind the log the user saw: transcription.rs:479 debug!("Audio vector length: {}") — that "N-million samples" line is the real in-RAM full-recording Vec entering (now chunked) ASR.
3. [LOW] Every audio channel is an unbounded std::mpsc — cpal callback → consumer (recorder.rs:181), consumer → PCM writer (session.rs:698), mic sensor (mic_sensor.rs:36). A stalled consumer (disk hang, wedged writer thread) silently queues ~64–192 KB/s per track with no backpressure or depth logging; 89 min of backlog ≈ 340 MB/track. Only pathological, but invisible when it happens.
B) The status lifecycle behind "11 failed rows, retry each by hand"
Status flow verified: save_pending_entry inserts 'transcribing' (history.rs:737-751) → finalize flips via update_transcription (history.rs:899-928); no-model → Failed with empty text (session.rs:916-919); startup fail_stale_transcribing (history.rs:274, 887). The no-model bug itself is fixed in the uncommitted ensure_model_ready diff.
1. [HIGH] Why there were 11 rows at all: auto-capture fragments a real meeting on every ~5 s quiet stretch. Once audio has been heard, presence = audio_idle < 800 ms (auto_capture.rs:174, 320-321), and 4 s of accumulated "absent" finalizes (STOP_AFTER, auto_capture.rs:172) — so any ≥ ~4.8 s pause where both tracks are below LOUD_RMS = 0.005 (session.rs:181) / mic-VAD-silent ends the session; the meeting app's still-open stream then re-triggers after the 8 s cooldown + 1.2 s debounce (auto_capture.rs:184, 170). An 89-min meeting with normal pauses becomes ~a dozen sessions, each boundary losing ≥ 9.2 s of audio — and under the (now-fixed) model bug, each fragment became one failed empty row. The fix in the diff stops the failures, not the fragmentation.
2. [MEDIUM] The retry path (manual and startup heal) silently drops speaker attribution. commands/history.rs:138 uses tm.transcribe(samples) — flat text only; it never calls the diarizer, never save_segments, and flips straight to Done (line 149-156). A healed meeting row permanently loses Me/Speaker labels; worse, segments persisted by a partially failed finalize (session.rs:929-933 saves segments before the status flip) stay attached and can contradict the new flat transcript.
3. [MEDIUM] The new startup heal re-runs full inference on legitimately-silent rows at EVERY launch. "No speech" is returned as Err (commands/history.rs:143-145), so the row stays failed+empty and failed_empty_entry_ids (uncommitted, history.rs:1232-1244) selects it again next launch → full WAV decode + full chunked inference per row per launch. The code comment "skipped again next launch in seconds" is wrong for long files; there is no "don't retry again" marker (e.g. a sentinel text or attempt count).
4. [LOW] Retry never flips the row to transcribing. No status write happens before inference, so during a (possibly minutes-long) heal or retry the row still reads failed; the DetailPane spinner is purely local component state (DetailPane.tsx:36,72). A user can fire a second manual retry of the same row the heal is already running — the inference_gate serializes them but both full runs execute.
5. [LOW] Failed-state affordance is per-row only, by design of the UI: the list row shows only a text suffix "· transcription failed" (ListRow.tsx:56); the retry button exists only in the DetailPane action bar (DetailPane.tsx:166-180) — select row, click retry, ×11. There is no retry-all anywhere in the UI; the uncommitted startup heal is launch-time only.
6. [LOW] resolve_status (session.rs:1025-1035) marks an empty-transcript, no-error session Done — such a row shows a bare timestamp title, and being Done it is excluded from the heal pass forever. Also: finalize writes the WAV before creating the row (finalize_session → archive_tracks → persist_session), so a crash in that window leaves archived audio with no history row ever — recovery's CleanupArchived (session.rs:566-572) assumes a row exists.
Frontend — CLEAN: HistorySettings.tsx:171-199 registers the historyUpdatePayload listener once (dep is a stable useCallback([])) with proper unlisten cleanup; SessionsSettings.tsx:46-65 likewise ([]), and the 250 ms elapsed interval is cleared on stop/unmount (69-89). No polling during recording; history is paged (30/page), overviews are one batched IPC per page, and audio playback uses convertFileSrc (OS-streamed) on macOS — only the Linux branch (HistorySettings.tsx:237-240) reads a whole WAV (~171 MB for 89 min) into a webview Blob. Entries hold only text; nothing in the UI holds recording-scale data during capture.
